Credit Risk Policy Summary
Synthetic evaluation document. No confidential data.

1. Credit Risk Framework
1.1 Northstar Retail Bank assigns credit risk grades A, B, C, D, and Watch.
1.2 Grade A means low expected loss, grade B means moderate expected loss, grade C means elevated expected loss, and grade D means high expected loss.
1.3 Watch status is used when payment behavior or collateral values require enhanced monitoring.
1.4 The Credit Risk Committee reviews portfolio trends every quarter.

2. Unsecured Personal Loans
2.1 The minimum bureau credit score for an unsecured personal loan is 660.
2.2 The maximum debt-to-income ratio is 42 percent.
2.3 Total unsecured personal loan exposure to one borrower may not exceed 75,000 USD.
2.4 Exceptions to the credit score floor require Credit Risk Director approval.
2.5 Loans with verified fraud indicators must be declined regardless of score.

3. Small Business Term Loans
3.1 Small business term loans require a debt service coverage ratio of at least 1.25.
3.2 The maximum loan-to-value ratio for owner-occupied commercial property collateral is 80 percent.
3.3 Borrowers must have risk grade B or better at origination.
3.4 Industry concentration above 18 percent in a single NAICS segment requires Credit Risk Committee review.
3.5 Environmental collateral issues must be escalated to the Collateral Risk Manager.

4. Reviews, Watchlist, and Exceptions
4.1 Watchlist reviews must be completed within 10 business days after a triggering event.
4.2 A triggering event includes 30 days past due, covenant breach, bankruptcy filing, or confirmed fraud investigation.
4.3 Exceptions below 5 percent of portfolio exposure may be approved by the Credit Risk Director.
4.4 Exceptions at or above 5 percent of portfolio exposure require Chief Credit Officer approval.
4.5 Model validation findings rated high must be remediated within 90 calendar days.
